Privacy Policy

Last updated: October 28, 2025

Important Notice

This Chrome extension ("Session Manager") is designed to help users manage their own browser session cookies for legitimate websites they have access to. This Privacy Policy comprehensively describes all data we collect, how we use it, how we store it, and with whom we share it.

1. Introduction

Swami Tools ("we", "our", or "us") operates the Session Manager Chrome Extension (the "Extension"). This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding the collection, use, storage, and disclosure of personal information when you use our Extension.

By using the Extension, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the Extension.

2. Data Collection - What We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of user data:

2.1 Personal Account Information

  • Username: Collected when you create an account to identify your account
  • Password: Collected and stored in hashed (encrypted) form only; we cannot see your actual password
  • Account Status: Information about your account type, plan status, plan expiration date, and whether you have administrative privileges

2.2 Device and Session Information

  • Unique Device ID: A randomly generated identifier to track which device you're using (format: ext_[random]_[timestamp])
  • Browser Information: User agent string, browser platform (Windows/Mac/Linux), browser type and version
  • Extension Version: The version number of the Extension you have installed
  • Session Tokens: Authentication tokens that prove your identity and maintain your logged-in session
  • Login Timestamps: Date and time when you log in, log out, or your session expires

2.3 Browser Cookies and Website Session Data

  • Session Cookies: The Extension accesses, reads, modifies, and stores browser cookies for websites you have been granted access to. This includes cookie names, values, domains, paths, expiration dates, and security flags (secure, httpOnly, sameSite)
  • Cookie Backups: If you choose to upload cookie backups to our servers, we store these cookie collections associated with your account
  • Website Permissions: A list of which websites you have been granted access to by administrators
  • Cookie Insertion Records: Logs of when you applied cookies to websites, including timestamps and which website

2.4 Browser Activity and Usage Data

  • Active Tab Information: URL, title, and tab ID of the currently active browser tab when you use the Extension
  • Feature Usage: Which Extension features you use (login, cookie insertion, cookie upload, etc.)
  • Extension Interactions: When you open the Extension popup, click buttons, or interact with Extension pages
  • Error Logs: Information about errors or failures when using the Extension

2.5 Chrome API Permissions Used

The Extension requests and uses the following Chrome permissions, which allow us to collect and access data:

  • cookies: Allows us to read, modify, and delete cookies for websites you access
  • storage: Allows us to store data locally on your device (tokens, settings, device ID)
  • tabs: Allows us to access information about your browser tabs and open/reload tabs
  • activeTab: Allows us to access the currently active tab's URL and reload it
  • host_permissions (https:///*/*): Allows us to access and modify cookies across all HTTPS websites

2.6 Data NOT Collected

For transparency, we want to clarify what we DO NOT collect:

  • We do NOT collect your browsing history beyond the current active tab when you use the Extension
  • We do NOT collect passwords from websites (only cookies)
  • We do NOT track your browsing activity outside of Extension usage
  • We do NOT collect payment card information directly (handled by third-party payment processors if applicable)
  • We do NOT sell or rent your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the collected data for the following specific purposes:

3.1 Core Functionality

  • Session Cookie Management: To read, store, apply, and manage browser cookies for authorized websites
  • User Authentication: To verify your identity and maintain secure logged-in sessions
  • Account Management: To create, manage, and maintain your user account
  • Access Control: To enforce which websites you are permitted to access and manage cookies for
  • Multi-Device Management: To track which devices you're logged in on and enforce device limits
  • Plan and Subscription Management: To track your subscription plan, expiration dates, and enforce plan limits

3.2 Service Improvement and Support

  • Technical Support: To diagnose and resolve technical issues you report
  • Service Optimization: To analyze how features are used and improve Extension performance
  • Compatibility Testing: To ensure the Extension works across different browsers and operating systems
  • Error Detection: To identify, log, and fix bugs or errors in the Extension

3.3 Security and Compliance

  • Security Monitoring: To detect and prevent unauthorized access or abuse
  • Session Validation: To verify session integrity and detect forced logouts
  • Compliance Enforcement: To ensure users comply with Terms of Service
  • Audit Logging: To maintain records of cookie insertions and access for security purposes

3.4 Communication

  • Service Notifications: To notify you about plan expiration, session expiry, or important service changes
  • Security Alerts: To alert you about unusual activity or forced logouts from other devices
  • Updates and Announcements: To inform you about new features or updates to the Extension

4. Data Storage and Security

We implement the following storage and security measures:

4.1 Local Storage on Your Device

  • Chrome Local Storage API: Session tokens, device IDs, user information, and Extension settings are stored locally on your device using Chrome's secure storage APIs (chrome.storage.local)
  • Cookie Data: The Extension stores cookies directly in your browser's cookie storage using Chrome's Cookies API
  • Access: This local data remains on your device and is only accessible to the Extension
  • Persistence: Local data persists until you uninstall the Extension or clear your browser data

4.2 Remote Server Storage

  • Server Location: Our backend server is hosted on Render.com (cloud hosting provider)
  • Server URL: https://swami-tools-server.onrender.com
  • Database: User accounts, cookie backups, and session data are stored in a MongoDB database hosted on MongoDB Atlas
  • Stored Data Includes:
    • User account information (username, hashed password, account status, plan details)
    • Cookie backups you choose to upload
    • Website permission assignments
    • Device session records
    • Activity logs (login times, cookie insertions)

4.3 Security Measures

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between the Extension and our servers uses HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Password Security: Passwords are hashed using industry-standard bcrypt hashing before storage; we cannot see your actual password
  • Authentication: We use JWT (JSON Web Tokens) for secure session authentication
  • Access Control: Server-side access controls limit who can access what data
  • Session Management: Sessions expire after a set period and are validated every 30 seconds
  • Device Limiting: We enforce device limits to prevent unauthorized account sharing

4.4 Data Security Limitations

While we implement reasonable security measures, please note:

  • No method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure
  • You are responsible for keeping your username and password confidential
  • Cookie data contains sensitive session information; ensure you only upload cookies for accounts you own

6. Data Retention - How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain different types of data for different periods:

6.1 Local Data on Your Device

  • Session Tokens and Settings: Retained indefinitely on your device until you:
    • Uninstall the Extension
    • Clear your browser data
    • Manually delete them through the Extension
  • Cookies: Retained in your browser's cookie storage until they expire or you delete them

6.2 Server-Stored Data

  • Active Account Data: Retained while your account is active and for as long as you continue using the service
  • Cookie Backups: Retained until you:
    • Manually delete them through the Extension
    • Request account deletion
    • Your account is terminated for Terms of Service violations
  • Session Records: Active session data is retained until:
    • You log out
    • Your session expires (typically 24-48 hours of inactivity)
    • You are logged out from another device
  • Activity Logs: Logs of cookie insertions and access may be retained for up to 90 days for security and troubleshooting purposes

6.3 After Account Deletion

When you request account deletion:

  • Immediate Deletion: Account credentials, cookie backups, and personal settings are deleted immediately
  • Log Data: Activity logs may be retained for up to 30 days for security auditing, then permanently deleted
  • Backup Systems: Data may persist in backup systems for up to 30 days before being permanently purged
  • Legal Retention: If legally required, certain data may be retained longer to comply with legal obligations

6.4 Inactive Accounts

  • Expired Plans: If your plan expires and you do not renew, cookie data is retained for 30 days to allow for renewal, then automatically deleted
  • Inactive Accounts: Accounts inactive for more than 1 year may be subject to deletion after email notification
